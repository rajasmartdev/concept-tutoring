WHAT IS NAPLAN

What is NAPLAN & Why It Matters

NAPLAN (National Assessment Program – Literacy and Numeracy) is taken by students in Years 3, 5, 7, and 9 across Australia.

Reading

Understanding & interpreting texts

Writing

Structured & creative expression

Language Conventions

Spelling, grammar & punctuation

Numeracy

Problem-solving & number skills

It's not a pass-or-fail test. It simply shows how your child is progressing and highlights areas where they may need support.

Understanding your child's strengths and areas for growth is the first step to meaningful improvement.
WHY PREPARE

Why Preparation Makes a Difference

Many students know the material but aren't always confident applying it under test conditions.

Our program focuses on helping them:

  • Work with greater accuracy and efficiency
  • Understand different question types
  • Build confidence through structured practice
OUR PROGRAM

How Our Program Works

We keep our approach simple, structured, and focused.

A structured 15-week program

We support students with NAPLAN preparation through a structured 15-week program that revises the full year's curriculum. Students work through carefully designed worksheets at different levels to build a clear understanding of key concepts. This helps them feel confident in their learning and well-prepared for the next school year.

Filling learning gaps

With this program, we focus on filling learning gaps so students feel more confident in their current work. We also introduce selected topics from the next year's curriculum, so they're not just catching up — they're getting ahead.

NAPLAN-style practice

Students complete four full-length online practice tests, helping them become familiar with exam conditions and feel more relaxed on the day.

Skills beyond content

We also support students with: Time management, Question analysis, Problem-solving strategies. Ongoing progress updates — You'll always know how your child is improving.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

FAQs

Does my child need NAPLAN preparation?

If they need more confidence or clarity in Maths or English, structured preparation can help.

When do we start?

Starting in October in the year prior works well, but earlier preparation allows more time to build confidence.

Is this only for struggling students?

Not at all. Many students use preparation to strengthen skills and aim for higher results.

How often are sessions?

1–2 sessions per week is usually effective.

Are online sessions effective?

Yes, they are flexible and just as effective as in-person sessions.

Will this reduce exam stress?

Yes. Familiarity with the format and regular practice helps students feel more at ease.
