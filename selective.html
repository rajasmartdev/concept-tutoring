Understanding the Selective Entry Test

That's What Makes Preparation So Important

Students who do well are usually those who have had time to understand the format, practise consistently, and build real confidence before the day arrives.

HOW WE HELP

How Concept Tutoring Can Help

Many students who are more than capable of earning a selective school place simply haven't had enough exposure to the type of thinking the exam demands. It's not a reflection of ability. It's a matter of preparation.

Through our Selective program, students work on:

Building strong analytical and reasoning skills
Developing the speed and accuracy needed under timed conditions
Feeling settled and prepared when the day arrives
Becoming familiar with the exam format through regular, authentic practice
Strengthening writing and structured response skills

These are skills not just for Selective Exam preparation but will be extended to when they go into VCE and beyond.

OUR APPROACH

Every Child Is Different

We don't believe in putting them all through the same process. We take the time to understand where each student is, and work from there.

1
Diagnostic Assessment

We start by understanding your child — where they're confident, where they need support, and how much ground there is to cover.

2
Parent Support Through the Process

We ensure the parents support their kids in the process, as the selective program requires consistency and perseverance both from the kids and parents.

3
Weekly Plan to Deliver Concepts

We have a weekly plan that takes the kids through all concepts from the Victorian curriculum — with basics practice to enforce concepts, then stretched into selective style questions.

4
Exam-Style Practice & Full Mock Tests

Students sit full model tests every five weeks under real conditions. By the time the exam comes, it feels familiar — not intimidating.

5
Skills + Strategy

Beyond content, we help students develop a calm approach to time management, strategies for complex questions, and the ability to think clearly when it matters most.

6
Regular Progress Updates

Progress is gradual — it takes 2–3 months for kids to get to a rhythm. We keep parents informed throughout, so you always have a clear picture of how your child is progressing.

FAQ

A Few Questions We Hear Often

1. Does my child really need tutoring for this?
+

Not every child needs tutoring, but the selective entry exam goes well beyond the school curriculum. Many capable students simply haven't been exposed to this style of thinking. Structured preparation helps them understand the format, build confidence, and perform closer to their actual potential.

2. How early should we start?
+

We recommend starting at least 6–12 months before the exam. Progress is gradual — it takes 2–3 months for students to find their rhythm. Starting early gives them time to build skills deeply rather than cram at the end.

3. My child is a strong student. Is this still worth it?
+

Absolutely. Strong students often benefit the most, because they have the capacity to reach high scores but may not be familiar with the exam's specific demands. Preparation helps them convert ability into performance on the day.

4. How often should sessions be?
+

We recommend once a week as a minimum, with regular independent practice in between. Consistency is what drives improvement — short, frequent practice is more effective than occasional intensive sessions.

5. Are online sessions just as effective?
+

Yes. Our online sessions are interactive and closely monitored. Students work through material in real time with their tutor, and we use digital resources that match the exam format. Many of our students attend fully online with excellent results.

6. My child gets quite anxious about exams. Will this help?
+

Yes — in fact, preparation is one of the best antidotes to exam anxiety. Familiarity with the format, regular mock tests, and time management strategies help students feel settled and in control when the real day arrives.
