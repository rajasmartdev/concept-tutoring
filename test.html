The Exam

Understanding the Selective Entry Test

Victoria's selective entry exam assesses students across five areas. It is designed to go well beyond what students cover at school — testing how a child thinks, not just what they've memorised. That's what makes preparation so important.

Mathematical Reasoning

Deep numerical thinking and problem solving that goes well beyond standard school mathematics.

Verbal Reasoning

Language-based logic tasks that test analytical thinking and the ability to reason through words.

Quantitative Analysis

Data interpretation, pattern recognition, and analytical reasoning with numbers and figures.

Reading Comprehension

Complex texts requiring inference, critical analysis, and deep understanding of the written word.

Writing

Structured, expressive responses demonstrating clarity, reasoning, and voice under time pressure.

Beyond Memorisation

Students who do well are those who've had time to understand the format, practise consistently, and build real confidence before the day arrives.

Our Approach

Every Child Is Different

We don't believe in putting all students through the same process. We take the time to understand where each student is, and work from there.

1
Diagnostic Assessment

We start by understanding your child — where they're confident, where they need support, and how much ground there is to cover.

2
Discuss Parent Support Through the Process

We ensure parents support their kids — the selective program requires consistency and perseverance from both students and family.

3
Weekly Plan to Deliver Concepts

A weekly plan takes students through all Victorian curriculum concepts — basics first, then stretched into selective-style questions.

4
Exam-Style Practice & Full Mock Tests

Students sit full model tests every five weeks under real conditions. By the time the exam comes, it feels familiar — not intimidating.

5
Skills + Strategy

Time management, working through unfamiliar questions, and the ability to think clearly when it matters most.

6
Regular Progress Updates

Progress takes 2–3 months to find a rhythm. We keep parents informed so you always have a clear picture of how your child is tracking.
